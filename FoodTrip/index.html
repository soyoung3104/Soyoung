北海道
Hokkaido

Hokkaido is also well known for fishing industry. Many tourists visit the island and enjoy fresh crab, urchin, scallop and other seafoods.

東京
Tokyo

Tokyo is the capital of Japan and the center of politics, economy and culture.

関東
Kanto

Making the most of its proximity to Tokyo, Chiba leads the nation in the production of several vegetables, including carrots, cabbages, and green onions.

名古屋・三重・静岡
Chubu

Nagoya city is known for its unique local Nagoya cuisine, called Nagoya-Meshi.
Mie is also known for its Japanese spiny lobster called Ise-ebi.
Shizuoka is well known for its tea production. Shizuoka tea is one of Japan’s three celebrated teas.

大阪
Osaka

Osaka has historically developed as a town of merchants. It has also been known as the nation’s kitchen.

京都
Kyoto

Kyoto is most well known for being the former Imperial capital of Japan for more than one thousand years. Kyoto still remains Japan’s cultural center.

奈良・神戸
Nara・Kobe

Nara was the capital of Japan from 710 to 784, and the Emperor lived there before moving the capital to Kyoto.
Kobe beef and Tajima beef produced in Hyogo are known as high-quality Wagyu beef.

広島
Hiroshima

Okonomi-Yaki, a kind of pancake containing a variety of ingredients, is famous local food of Hiroshima. Okonomi means “how you like” or “what you like”.

香川・愛媛
Shikoku

Sanuki-Udon noodles is the most famous local food of Kagawa. There are over 700 udon restaurants in Kagawa prefecture.
Ehime is famous for its citrus fruits such as Mikan and Iyokan. They are similar in appearance to a mandarin orange.

福岡
Fukuoka

Fukuoka is well known for its local food. Especially, Hakata-Ramen is popular with tourists.

長崎・大分・熊本
Kyushu

Nagasaki is famous for its local noodles. Especially, Champon noodles and Sara-Udon noodles. Yufuin-onsen is also popular hot spring in Oita.
Kumamoto is well known for its Kumamoto ramen noodles in pork broth soup.

沖縄
Okinawa

Okinawa is a major producer of sugar cane, pineapple, papaya, and other tropical fruit.
